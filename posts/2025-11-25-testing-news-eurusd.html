Exploring High-Impact News and Post-News Strategy

Nov 25, 2025 — Ali Abbasi Sardari

Step 1: Collecting News Data

I focused on the economic calendar and merged EUR/USD daily price data with a binary indicator for high‑impact news events. This allowed me to isolate the days where market reactions should be strongest.

Step 2: Testing Machine Learning on News

I trained a machine learning model to see if high‑impact news could predict EUR/USD moves. The idea was to measure whether news days left a consistent footprint in returns. Unfortunately, the model didn’t find meaningful patterns — the market’s noise overwhelmed the signal.

Step 3: Backtesting the Post-News Strategy

Later in the evening, I switched to backtesting a trading strategy built around news candles:

  • On a news day, measure the candle’s body.
  • Place a pending order at 25% of that body size away from the close.
  • Stop loss at the signal day’s high or low.
  • Take profit at 1.5R.
  • Only allow the order to trigger on the next day; cancel if not triggered.

Despite repairing the dataset (open = previous close), the backtest still had bugs. Some trades that should have triggered — like the first January news day — weren’t showing up. This suggests either the dataset still has issues or the trigger logic needs refining.

Lessons Learned

  • High‑impact news alone may not provide a clean predictive edge.
  • Data integrity is critical — even small errors in OHLC values can invalidate backtests.
  • Strategy rules must be coded carefully to match real chart behavior.
  • Failed attempts are still progress: they highlight what needs fixing next.
