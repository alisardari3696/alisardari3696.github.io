December USD News Trading Backtest – 3-Phase Breakdown

Feb 8, 2026 — Ali Abbasi Sardari

Overview

I backtested USD high-impact economic news releases on EURUSD during December, using a news-driven strategy filtered by VWAP. The objective was to evaluate whether the strategy has an edge, the impact of fixed Stop Loss / Take Profit, and how different SL/TP distances affect performance. All results include real trading costs (spread + commission).

Trading Rules (Same for All Phases)

These rules were identical across all three phases:

  • High-impact USD news only (employment, inflation, PMI, GDP, etc.)
  • 1-hour candle confirmation — wait for the 1-hour candle to close after the news
  • No immediate entries during volatility spikes
  • VWAP direction filter — trades only in the direction of VWAP; no counter-VWAP trades
  • Entry executed after 1-hour close if price and VWAP aligned (direction based on news result + VWAP bias)
  • Manual trade management: if TP or SL not hit, trades closed before daily rollover; if a 1-hour candle closed opposite VWAP, trade closed; no overnight trades

Phase 1 – No Fixed SL / TP (Manual Exits)

Setup: No fixed stop or target; trades managed manually using VWAP and price behavior.

Results:

  • Net result: +15.5 pips
  • Win rate: ~64%
  • Large losses occurred; high drawdown and inconsistent outcomes

Observation: Large losing trades erased multiple winning trades.

Phase 2 – Fixed SL = 15 pips / TP = 20 pips

What changed: Fixed risk management added (SL = 15 pips, TP = 20 pips). All other rules unchanged.

Results:

  • Net result: +23.8 pips
  • Maximum loss capped at −15 pips
  • Improved stability and consistency

Observation: Risk control alone improved performance.

Phase 3 – Fixed SL = 15 pips / TP = 15 pips

What changed (vs Phase 2): Only the TP distance was reduced to 15 pips; SL remained 15 pips. Same entries, same timing, same management rules.

Results:

  • Net result: +40.0 pips
  • Same win rate
  • Lower drawdown
  • More trades completed within session range

Why Phase 3 worked better (interpretation): SL and TP matched closer to the 1-hour ATR. Most post-news moves complete within one hour; price often loses momentum or consolidates after the initial reaction. Matching SL/TP to real post-news volatility improved execution and consistency.

Performance Comparison — Net Pips (December, after fees)

Phase 1  | +15.5

Key Takeaways

  • Waiting for the 1-hour candle close reduced noise
  • VWAP alignment filtered low-quality setups
  • Fixed SL reduced drawdown immediately
  • TP placement must match volatility
  • Most news moves respect the 1-hour ATR
  • Execution discipline is critical

Final Conclusion

The most effective setup in December was: high-impact USD news, 1-hour candle confirmation, VWAP-aligned entries, SL = 15 pips / TP = 15 pips, and no overnight trades. This configuration aligned best with post-news market behavior and produced the strongest results.
